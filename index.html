Plan arranged chronologically — from creation (c. 2000 BC) to the end of the Persian period (c. 400 BC). Each stage includes narrative and prophetic books read in parallel with the historical storyline.
c. 2000–1700 BC
Stage 1 — Prehistory and Patriarchs
Geography: Ur of the Chaldeans, Harran, Canaan (Hebron, Bethel, Shechem), Egypt
Reading order
Gen 1–11 Gen 12–25 Gen 26–36 Gen 37–50 Job
What we read and why
Gen 1–11: creation, fall, flood, Babel — the foundation of theology. Gen 12–50: Abraham (Ur→Canaan), Isaac, Jacob (12 tribes), Joseph (Egypt). The book of Job — probably from this era; read it after Gen 3 as a meditation on suffering.
c. 1280–1200 BC
Stage 2 — Exodus, Wilderness, Conquest of Canaan
Geography: Egypt (Goshen, Ramesses), Sinai, Moab, Jericho, Canaan tribe by tribe
Reading order
Ex 1–20 Lev (selected) Num 10–36 Deut 1–11; 27–34 Josh Judg 1–3 Ruth
Reading tip
From Lev read ch. 1, 16, 19, 23 — the core of the Law. Num 10–14 (the journey) and 20–21 (end of wandering). Deuteronomy contains Moses' farewell speeches — read it as a summary. Ruth fits right after Judges — geographically: Bethlehem in Judah.
c. 1200–1050 BC
Stage 3 — Judges and Unification
Geography: each judge comes from a different region — Dan (north), Ephraim (centre), Judah (south), Transjordan
Reading order
Judg 4–21 1 Sam 1–8
Judges and tribal geography
Deborah (Ephraim/Naphtali), Gideon (Manasseh), Jephthah (Gilead, east of Jordan), Samson (Dan). Keep a tribal map open alongside each judge — it is the key to understanding Israel's divisions. Samuel closes the era and opens the monarchy.
c. 1050–930 BC
Stage 4 — United Kingdom
Geography: Gibeah (Saul), Hebron (David), Jerusalem (capital), Zion, Solomon's Temple
Reading order
1 Sam 9–31 2 Sam 1–24 Psalms of David 1 Kgs 1–11 Prov Eccl Song
Psalms woven into the narrative
Ps 18 after 2 Sam 22; Ps 51 after 2 Sam 12 (David's sin); Ps 3 after 2 Sam 15 (flight from Absalom). Psalm headings are chronological signposts. Prov, Eccl and Song — read after 1 Kgs 3–11 (Solomon).
930–722 BC
Stage 5 — Divided Kingdom (Israel and Judah)
Geography: Samaria (northern capital of Israel), Jerusalem (southern capital of Judah), Assyria advancing from the north
Read in parallel — Israel and Judah
1 Kgs 12–22 2 Kgs 1–17 Amos (Israel) Hos (Israel) Jonah Joel (Judah) Isa 1–39 (Judah) Mic (Judah)
Synchronisation key
For each king in 1–2 Kgs, insert the prophet active during that reign. Elijah and Elisha are built into the text of Kings. The stage ends with the fall of Samaria (2 Kgs 17) — deportation of the 10 northern tribes by Assyria in 722 BC.
722–586 BC
Stage 6 — Judah Alone
Geography: Jerusalem, Lachish, Egypt (ally), Babylon advancing from the north-east
Reading order
2 Kgs 18–25 2 Chr 29–36 Isa 40–55 Nah Zeph Hab Jer 1–45 Lam
Prophets in chronological order
Nah — fall of Nineveh (612); Zeph and Hab — under Josiah and Jehoiakim. Jer — from Josiah's reform (621) to the destruction of Jerusalem (586). Read Lamentations immediately after 2 Kgs 25 — an elegy over the ruined city.
586–400 BC
Stage 7 — Babylonian Exile and Return
Geography: Babylon (Ezek, Dan), Persia — Susa (Esth), return to Judah and rebuilding of Jerusalem
Reading order
Ezek (Babylon) Dan (Babylon) Esth (Persia) Ezra 1–6 Hag Zech 1–8 Ezra 7–10 Neh Isa 56–66 Zech 9–14 Mal
Chronological key
Ezekiel and Daniel write from Babylon during the exile. Hag and Zech 1–8 — at the rebuilding of the Temple (520–515 BC). Ezra 7 and Neh — 457–444 BC, the second wave of return. Mal and Zech 9–14 — c. 430 BC — the closing of the OT canon. The Old Testament ends with an unanswered question — answered by the New Testament.
